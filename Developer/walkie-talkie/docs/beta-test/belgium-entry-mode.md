## Executive Summary

For a pre-revenue SaaS with a 0-employee model, Belgium entry mode is straightforward: **direct digital sales with no physical presence**. This is the only rational choice at current stage. The analysis below maps all options, explains why alternatives fail, and defines what "entry" actually means for Mesh operationally.

---

## 1. Entry Mode Options Evaluated

### Option A: Direct Digital Sales (Recommended)
Sell directly via trymesh.chat. Belgian customers sign up, pay via Stripe, use the product. No Belgian entity, no local office, no local employees.

**Pros:**
- Zero overhead — no incorporation, no local payroll, no office lease
- Already operational — the product exists and Stripe processes Belgian cards today
- Consistent with 0-employee model
- EU single market means no trade barriers for digital goods

**Cons:**
- No local presence for enterprise sales conversations
- Cannot host in-person events or demo days without travel
- Some large Belgian enterprises prefer local vendor relationships (procurement bias)

**Verdict:** The only viable option at current stage. All other options require capital, legal entities, or employees — none of which Mesh has or wants.

---

### Option B: Local Partnership / Reseller
Partner with a Belgian IT consultancy or SaaS reseller who sells Mesh to their client base on commission.

**Pros:**
- Local relationships and language capability
- Partner handles enterprise procurement complexity
- No direct legal entity needed

**Cons:**
- Revenue share reduces margin (typically 20–30%)
- Partner quality is hard to vet remotely
- Dependent on partner's motivation and pipeline
- Requires legal partnership agreement + support SLA
- Not compatible with self-serve SaaS growth model

**Verdict:** Premature. Revisit at €50K+ ARR when enterprise sales require a local champion.

---

### Option C: Belgian Legal Entity (BV/NV)
Incorporate a Belgian subsidiary. Opens local bank account, registers for Belgian VAT, hires local staff.

**Pros:**
- Full local credibility for enterprise/public sector deals
- Enables Belgian government grants (e.g., VLAIO subsidies, Innoviris)
- Simplifies VAT for large Belgian customers

**Cons:**
- Incorporation cost: €2,000–5,000 + legal fees
- Ongoing accounting: €3,000–6,000/year
- **Violates Mesh's 0-employee model** — any Belgian entity with employees triggers Belgian labor law, social security, etc.
- Not required for digital services under EU e-commerce law

**Verdict:** Never, at least not with employees. A shell BV for VAT purposes only is theoretically possible but adds complexity with minimal benefit given EU OSS scheme.

---

### Option D: Acquisition / Merger
Acquire a Belgian SaaS tool or team to gain instant local market presence.

**Pros:**
- Immediate customer base and market knowledge
- Local team handles sales and support

**Cons:**
- Requires capital Mesh does not have
- Acquired team = employees, which breaks the model
- Irrelevant at current stage

**Verdict:** Not applicable.

## 2. What "Entry" Actually Means for Mesh

For a digital-native, 0-employee company, "market entry" is not a legal or physical event. It is a **customer acquisition and positioning event**.

Belgium entry = the moment Mesh has:
1. A Belgian company paying for a Pro or Team subscription
2. A case study from a Belgian AI team using Mesh
3. Belgian-language landing page variant live

None of these require incorporation, partnerships, or physical presence.

## 4. Competitive Landscape in Belgium

**Direct competitors in the Belgian market for AI coordination tools:** None identified at this scale.

**Adjacent competitors:**
| Tool | Description | Weakness vs Mesh |
|---|---|---|
| Slack | Team chat, no AI-native coordination | Not built for agent-to-agent work |
| Linear | Task management | No real-time AI agent presence |
| Notion | Docs + projects | No live agent coordination |
| Microsoft Teams | Enterprise comms | Heavy, expensive, no AI-native layer |

**Belgian AI ecosystem entry points:**
- **imec** — Belgium's leading nanotech/digital research hub. Partners with AI startups.
- **Becode.org** — AI & data training org. Their graduates are Mesh's users.
- **Startit @ KBC** — startup incubator backed by largest Belgian bank. Free visibility if accepted.
- **Agoria** — Belgian tech industry federation. Enterprise contacts.
